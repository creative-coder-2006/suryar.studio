Project 02 / 03

Iron Clutch

An automobile enthusiast shop with a cult following. Replatformed their digital presence to match the precision and obsession of the community they serve.

ScopeWeb Design, Systems
Year2025
DeliverablesWebsite, Product System, Brand Refresh
RoleDesign Direction & Execution

The Brief

Iron Clutch had something rare — a genuinely devoted community of automobile enthusiasts who found each other through their shop. Modified cars, aftermarket parts, restoration projects, weekend meets. The culture was real and specific.

Their website was not. It had been cobbled together, suffered five years of neglect, and felt like it belonged to a different business entirely. The community didn't care — they came for the parts and the people. But the business was leaving reach on the table.

The brief: build something obsessive, precise, and built for people who care. No lifestyle photography. No stock cars. Just craft, catalogued with the same attention to detail that their customers bring to their builds.

Design Principles

Built for people who notice everything.

Dark-first UI

Designed primarily for night browsing. The colour system uses carbon, graphite, and bare metal tones — pulled directly from the material language of the workshop.

Catalogue Precision

Every part page has spec tables, compatibility guides, and fitment notes. Information density is high — but the hierarchy is so clean it never feels overwhelming.

Community First

The site's secondary purpose is a build diary hub. Members document their projects, link the parts they used, and build a living catalogue of real-world applications.

Product System

Every spec, in its right place.

The product page template was redesigned from scratch. Part number, compatibility matrix, weight, material, finish — all surfaced without clutter. A typographic system rigorous enough for engineers, clean enough for enthusiasts.

Brand Refresh

The logo was the last thing we touched.

The mark — a stylised clutch disc — had been with them for years. The community recognised it. We refined the geometry, tightened the letterforms, and built a type system around it. Nothing you'd notice. Everything you'd feel.

Outcomes

What actually changed.

01

Digital Sales

Online part orders, previously negligible, became a meaningful revenue stream within two months of launch. The catalogue finally worked as a sales tool, not just a list.

02

Community Growth

The build diary feature drove a significant uptick in account registrations. Members now had a reason to be on the site beyond purchasing.

03

Brand Legitimacy

The refresh gave the shop credibility that matched what the community already knew — that Iron Clutch was serious about their craft.
